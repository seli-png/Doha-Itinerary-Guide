Guide for Moving to Qatar

Introduction

Qatar, a small yet influential nation in the Gulf, is a hub of cultural diversity, modern infrastructure, and exceptional opportunities. Known for its robust economy, world-class healthcare, and high standard of living, Qatar attracts individuals and families worldwide. This guide will walk you through the essential information and processes for relocating to Qatar, tailored for families, individuals, and professionals from different parts of the globe.

General Overview of Qatar

Location:

Qatar is a peninsula in the Arabian Gulf, strategically located near key international shipping routes. It shares its only land border with Saudi Arabia to the south and is surrounded by the Arabian Gulf on three sides. Its central location makes it a convenient gateway between Europe, Asia, and Africa.

Climate:

Qatar experiences a desert climate characterized by hot summers (May-September) where temperatures can soar above 45°C (113°F), and mild, pleasant winters (October-April) with temperatures averaging 20°C (68°F). Rainfall is sparse, with occasional showers during the cooler months.

Language:

Arabic is the official language, but English is widely used in business, education, and daily life, making it easy for expatriates to adapt. Other languages like Hindi, Urdu, Tagalog, and Malayalam are also commonly spoken due to the diverse expatriate population.

Currency:

The Qatari Riyal (QAR) is pegged to the US Dollar, providing stability. ATMs and card payments are widely accessible, and banking services are efficient for residents and visitors alike.

Culture:

Rooted in Islamic traditions, Qatar's culture emphasizes family values, respect, and hospitality. Despite its conservative nature, the country is welcoming to other cultures due to its large expatriate population, which constitutes over 80% of its residents. Traditional practices such as falconry, dhow sailing, and camel racing coexist with a modern, cosmopolitan lifestyle.

Key Attractions:

  • Museum of Islamic Art: Showcasing Islamic artifacts from across the globe.
  • Souq Waqif: A vibrant marketplace offering traditional goods, spices, and dining experiences.
  • Aspire Park: A lush green space ideal for families and outdoor activities.
  • The Pearl-Qatar: A man-made island with luxury residences, restaurants, and retail outlets.
  • Lusail City: A futuristic city featuring state-of-the-art architecture, entertainment hubs, and venues like the Lusail Iconic Stadium, a centerpiece of the 2022 FIFA World Cup.
  • Desert Adventures: The inland sea (Khor Al Adaid) is a UNESCO-recognized natural reserve and a popular spot for dune bashing and camping.

  • Visa and Residency Requirements ▼

    • Visa and Residency Information

    For Individuals:

    • Work Visa: This is the most common type of visa for individuals relocating to Qatar. It must be secured through an employer, who acts as your sponsor. A formal job offer is required before applying.
      • Steps:
        • The employer submits a visa application on your behalf via the Ministry of Interior (MOI) website.
        • Documents required:
          • Passport copy
          • Employment contract
          • Passport-sized photographs
          • Educational certificates (attested)
        • Once the application is approved, the employee must complete a medical examination and fingerprinting.
      • Processing Time: 2-4 weeks.
    • Family Sponsorship: Individuals earning above QAR 10,000 per month are eligible to sponsor their immediate family members.
      • Documents Required:
        • Proof of income (salary certificate or bank statement)
        • Valid tenancy agreement
        • Marriage certificate (attested)
        • Children’s birth certificates (attested)
      • Application through the Hukoomi portal.
    • Student Visa: Issued to those enrolling in recognized Qatari institutions such as Qatar University or Hamad Bin Khalifa University. Sponsored by the educational institution.
      • Documents Required:
        • Acceptance letter from the institution
        • Passport copy and photographs
        • Proof of financial stability

    For Families:

    • Residency Permit (RP): The working member of the family sponsors dependents through an RP.
    • Dependent Visas: Spouses and children under 18 must hold dependent visas.
      • Documents Required:
        • Marriage and birth certificates (attested)
        • Valid passports for all family members
        • Tenancy agreement showing adequate housing
        • Employment contract of the sponsor
      • Applications are made through the MOI website.

    Steps for Obtaining a Visa:

    1. Online Application: Submit the necessary forms via the Ministry of Interior or Hukoomi portal.
    2. Health Check: Undergo mandatory medical tests, including:
      • Blood tests for infectious diseases (e.g., HIV, Hepatitis B)
      • Chest X-ray for tuberculosis
    3. Biometric Enrollment: Fingerprints and photos must be submitted at the biometric centers.
    4. Final Approval: Once all requirements are met, collect your visa or residency permit from the designated MOI service center.

    Health Requirements:

    • Medical Tests: Mandatory health screenings must be completed at designated health centers such as Al Khor Medical Center or Doha Health Center.
    • Vaccinations:
      • Hepatitis A: Protection against diseases transmitted through contaminated food and water.
      • Tetanus: Essential for safeguarding against bacterial infections.
      • MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella) and Polio vaccines are also advised.

    Apps and Tools that are Needed:

    • Ehteraz: A mandatory health app for tracking COVID-19 and vaccination status.
    • Hukoomi: The government’s online portal for visa applications and related services.
    • Metrash2: Useful for checking visa status, traffic violations, and other residency services.

    Locations to Keep in Mind:

    • Medical Screening: Visit approved centers such as Al Khor or Doha Health Center for medical tests.
    • Biometric Centers: The MOI Service Centers in Al Gharrafa or Industrial Area are popular options.
    • Visa Collection: MOI offices in Mesaimeer or Madinat Khalifa handle visa finalizations.
